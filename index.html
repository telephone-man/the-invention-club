The right level of support for each age group.

The same idea stretches from playful cause-and-effect making to robotics, debugging and design choices. Initial sessions will launch for selected age groups while the wider model is developed.

Age Group Support level Typical making
4-6Mini InventorsAdult-supportedStories, big parts and cause-and-effect play
6-8Junior InventorsGuided makingButtons, wheels, switches and simple structures
8-10½InventorsSupported choicesMotors, sensors, stronger builds and simple logic
10½-14Advanced InventorsMore self-directedRobotics, radio control, debugging and trade-offs
14-18Studio InventorsIndependent studio workOpen builds, mentoring, repairability and deeper choices

Curious how the making is guided?

Sessions feel playful and child-led, but there is a simple rhythm underneath: try one practical idea, combine it with others, then use it in a real invention.

Practical questions before joining.

Does my child need coding experience?

No. Coding is introduced as one invention material, alongside motors, sensors, structures, craft materials and testing.

Which age groups are available?

The full age range being explored is 4-18, but initial sessions will launch for selected age groups first.

Is it safe?

Activities should use age-appropriate materials, supervised tools and safe batteries. Full safety, safeguarding and consent arrangements will be confirmed before launch.

Is this school-like?

It has structure, but the feel is workshop-first: make, test, improve, share.

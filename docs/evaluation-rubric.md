# Evaluation rubric

The schema passes if:
- At least 80% of power cards have one clear primary family.
- At least 80% of levels stay mostly inside their family.
- Integration cards can absorb most overlap cases cleanly.
- A facilitator could understand the distinction between skill, integration and invention.
- Generated examples feel usable in a real weekly club.
- The report identifies weaknesses without needing to rewrite the whole schema.

The schema fails if:
- More than 30% of useful cards feel arbitrarily classified.
- The level ladder cannot apply across most families.
- Cross-family integrations become so dominant that skill families stop being useful.
- The schema requires lots of exceptions.
- The final model is too complex for a children’s invention club.

A borderline result should be labelled:
- Viable with amendments
